## Peer-Reviewed & Formal Research

### Staggered Designer Multistep Methods: Development and Analysis

**Date:** 2021  
**Type:** Undergraduate Research Project  
**Advisor:** Dr. Michelle Ghrist, Gonzaga University  

Research on novel temporal discretization schemes for solving systems of differential equations. Focused on developing multistep methods with improved stability regions and convergence properties.

**Key Contributions:**
- Development of staggered designer multistep method variants
- Rigorous stability analysis and error bounds
- Comparison with established multistep methods
- Applications to stiff ODE systems

**Related Repository:** [staggered-multistep](https://github.com/caleb-cramer/staggered-multistep)

## Technical Reports & Course Projects

### Wildfire Spread Modeling: A Coupled Reaction-Diffusion-Convection System

**Date:** 2025  
**Type:** Course Project - AMATH 581 Scientific Computing  
**Institution:** University of Washington  

Comprehensive study of wildfire propagation using coupled partial differential equations. Bridges theoretical numerical analysis with practical environmental applications.

**Key Topics:**
- Reaction-diffusion-convection equations for fire dynamics
- Finite difference and finite element discretization
- Stability analysis and convergence verification
- Numerical simulations with visualization

**Related Repository:** [581FinalProject](https://github.com/caleb-cramer/581FinalProject)

### Online Learning for Sports Analytics: Dueling Bandits in ATP Tennis

**Date:** 2026  
**Type:** Course Project - CSE 541 Interactive Learning  
**Institution:** University of Washington  

Application of online learning algorithms to professional tennis prediction. Demonstrates practical implementation of dueling bandit frameworks with real-world temporal data.

**Key Topics:**
- Dueling bandit algorithms and preference learning
- Head-to-head match prediction models
- Adaptive learning in competitive environments
- Performance evaluation on historical match data

**Related Repository:** [Dueling-Bandits-for-ATP-Tennis](https://github.com/caleb-cramer/Dueling-Bandits-for-ATP-Tennis)
